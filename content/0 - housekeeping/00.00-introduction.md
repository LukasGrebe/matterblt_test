## How it is organized


The first number is the **chapter** (the broad area the document belongs to). The second is the **section** within that chapter. This is a lightweight adaptation of [Johnny.Decimal](https://johnnydecimal.com).

Chapters are defined in `config/chapter-registry.yaml`. There is no universal chapter list — you replace the default with whatever areas matter for your team. Chapter `00` is always Housekeeping: documents about the system itself, of which this is the first.

Every document also declares a **kind** — one of `tutorial`, `how-to`, `reference`, or `explanation`. Kind answers the question *what job does this document do*, independent of what it is about. A reference document and a how-to guide on the same topic are different documents because they serve different reader needs.

## What to change when you fork this

* what three words * 

Three files define everything domain-specific:

| File | What it controls |
|---|---|
| `config/chapter-registry.yaml` | Your chapters: their IDs, names, and descriptions |
| `config/layer-registry.yaml` | Your layer axis: what the layers mean and how they relate |
| `config/site.yaml` | Project name, base URL, and RAG chunking settings |

Everything else — the frontmatter schema, the validation script, the Eleventy build, the document templates, the CI workflow — works unchanged regardless of domain.

Start by replacing the chapter registry. The rest follows.

---

## What this is not

This is not a wiki. Documents here are not pages to be continuously edited into an ever-growing single source of truth. Each document has a specific kind and a specific scope. When something changes, you write a new document or update a reference — you do not blur a how-to guide with an explanation because both happened to be about the same topic.

It is also not a task tracker, a meeting notes dump, or a project management tool. Those have their place elsewhere. This system is for knowledge that needs to survive beyond the project, the sprint, or the person who originally held it.
